# The Vidyard Alternative That Skips the Per-Seat Subscription

Vidyard is built for sales teams — hosting, analytics, CRM integrations, and per-seat pricing to match. But if what you actually need is to **record your screen and camera, trim, and export a clean file**, you're paying for a platform when you want a tool.

**BloomRecorder** is that tool. **$29 one-time** for the installer (or free from MIT-licensed source), **100% local**, with recordings saved to your own disk. No seats, no cloud, no subscription.

## Why people look past Vidyard

- **Per-seat, per-month pricing.** Vidyard Pro lists around **$19/user/mo** (annual), with the Starter tier at **$59/mo** and team tiers climbing from there.
- **It's a hosting platform first.** You're buying cloud hosting and analytics you may not need just to record a clip.
- **Recordings live on their servers.** Storage and playback are tied to your plan.
- **Free tier is capped.** Video limits push you toward paid quickly.
- **Overkill for simple capture.** A lot of the value is in features a solo user or small team never touches.

## How BloomRecorder is different

- **$29 once — or free from source.** One-time installer via Whop, or build the MIT-licensed code yourself.
- **100% local:** zero telemetry, zero network calls, zero accounts.
- **Recordings save to your own disk** in an output folder you choose.
- **Unlimited recording length** — no plan caps.
- **Record screen, camera, or both** — split files or picture-in-picture combined.
- **Source picker with live thumbnails**; device selection for camera and mic.
- **System + mic audio mixed into every output.**
- **Floating always-on-top recording pill** with timer + stop.
- **Local library with auto-thumbnails.**
- **MP4 export** (H.264, faststart) and **optimized two-pass GIF export** (palettegen/paletteuse).
- **Trim before export.**
